## Validation Checking

For each country, I got the larges plant and validated the estimated heat demand from published literature. 

Austria: 
- Ybbstaler Zellstoff Pulp Mill: 343,176 MWh
- "40% was fossil fuels (e.g., coal, oil, natural gas) and 60% was biofuels"
- Source: Rahnama Mobarakeh, M., Santos Silva, M., & Kienberger, T. (2021). Pulp and paper industry: Decarbonisation technology assessment to reach CO2 neutral emissions—An Austrian case study. Energies, 14(4), 1161. https://doi.org/10.3390/en14041161
- "According to the Rahnama Mobarakeh et al. (2021) study, Austria's pulp and paper sector relies on fossil fuels for roughly 40% of its thermal energy needs, with the remaining 60% covered by internal biomass like black liquor. Applying this industry-standard fuel split to the paper's chemical pulping process energy demands, this plant's value translates to a highly realistic energy footprint of 1.32 MWh of fossil heat per ton of pulp. This matches the study's national market averages and process benchmarks."
